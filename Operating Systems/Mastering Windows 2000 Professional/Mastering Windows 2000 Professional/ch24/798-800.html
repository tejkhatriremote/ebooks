Avoiding the Guest Account Conundrum

The last issue we want to look at is a security issue, and it has to do with the group called Everyone and an account called Guest. Windows 2000 builds the two accounts on your system. The Guest account can be used as a generic logon account, instead of building accounts for occasional visitors or employees.

You cannot administer the Everyone account through the User and Groups console because Windows 2000 literally assumes that Everyone means—well, everyone. Everyone would include the Guest account. Therefore, if you enable the Guest account, the guest will have access to everything that the Everyone group has access to. This situation could easily lead to a breakdown in security. Fortunately the guest account is disabled by default.
