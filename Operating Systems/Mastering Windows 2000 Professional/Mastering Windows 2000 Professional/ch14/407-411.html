Creating a Cover Page

If you’re in business today, a fax machine is as essential as an office copier or a coffeepot. The most common tool for facsimile communication is still the stand-alone fax machine, but there are some real advantages to using your PC as a personal fax machine instead.

To send a fax through a fax machine you need to: create the document; print a copy on paper; write up a cover sheet; carry the document and cover sheet to the fax machine; wait for your turn to use the machine; dial the recipient’s fax number; feed the document and cover sheet through the fax machine; and finally, walk back to your office. When somebody sends you a fax, it spills out of the machine and waits for you to come get it—meanwhile, everybody who walks past the fax machine has a chance to read your messages before you do.

On the other hand, you can send and receive faxes through your PC and a fax modem without leaving your chair. If you have a home office or a really small business with a tight budget, you can do PC-based faxing without spending a couple of hundred dollars or more for a separate machine. And when you’re traveling with a portable computer, you can use it to exchange faxes with your office and your customers.

In the following sections, you will learn how to use Windows 2000’s Fax Service to send and receive faxes from your PC just as if you were printing the document or sending e-mail. You’ll also learn how to create personalized, custom cover pages for your faxes.

Installing Fax Service

Windows 2000 makes installation of the Fax Service almost ridiculously easy. All you need to do is install and configure a fax-capable modem in your computer, and Windows 2000 will automatically install the Fax Service and tools.

To install a modem in Windows 2000, follow the manufacturer’s instructions for the hardware installation, and then use the following steps:

1.  Click the Start button, and open the Control Panel from the Settings menu.
2.  If you’re using an external modem, turn it on and make sure it’s connected to both the telephone line and a serial port on your computer.
3.  Double-click the Phone and Modem Options icon. The Phone and Modem Options dialog box in Figure 14.1 appears.


Figure 14.1  The Phone and Modem Options dialog box lets you install a new modem.

4.  Click the Modems tab, and then click the Add button to open the Install New Modem Wizard.
5.  Because you turned on the modem in step 2, you can let the Install New Modem Wizard try to identify your modem type. Click the Next button.
6.  If you have an external modem, you will see the lights flash on the front panel while the wizard tests it. When the tests are complete, the wizard displays the final dialog box.
7.  If the wizard is not able to identify your modem, you will be prompted to choose its make and model from the list. If your modem is not listed, look in the modem manual for an equivalent type, or select the Generic Modem Drivers item in the Manufacturers list. Windows 2000 supports most modems on the market today, but also allows for new models that will inevitably be released in the future. If you own a modem that is not listed, contact the vendor for updated drivers, or check the disk that was provided with the modem. In this case, use the Have Disk button to install the driver.
8.  Click the Next and Finish buttons to complete the modem installation. If you haven’t already entered your location and area code, the Location Information dialog box shown in Figure 14.2 asks for this information.


Figure 14.2  The Location Information dialog box prompts you to enter your TAPI location settings.

9.  If your modem is connected to a line that uses Touch-Tone dialing, choose Tone. If not, choose Pulse.

You should discover that the Fax Service has been installed once the modem installation is complete. Windows 2000 will install the Fax folder on the Start menu under Programs Accessories Communications Fax.

Configuring Windows 2000 Fax

Most of the configuration of the Windows 2000 Fax Service is done through the Control Panel Fax applet, shown in Figure 14.3. This applet contains the basic information that Windows 2000 will use to fill in your cover page, as well as your list of personal cover pages and other service settings.


Figure 14.3  The User Information tab of Fax Properties contains your personal fax information.

To open the Fax Properties, click Start Settings Control Panel and double-click the Fax icon.

User Information

The User Information tab contains the basic information that will populate your cover page when you send a fax. If has fields for your name, fax number, address, company name, and so on.

You don’t have to fill in every single field on the tab, but the more you fill out, the more complete your cover page will be. At least, the more complete it will be without you having to fill it in every time.

Most businesses would consider the minimum information to be the person’s name, company name, and fax number. You can decide how much information is enough.
