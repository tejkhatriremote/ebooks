The Windows 2000 Resource Kits

With the release of NT 4, Microsoft separated the NT Resource Kit into two parts. There is now a Resource Kit for NT Workstation and one for NT Server. They both cover different material and are targeted at the specific type of environment that the operating systems will be used in. This philosophy will most likely continue with Windows 2000 due to the sheer size of the information being provided.

The Workstation Resource Kit covers the basics of installation, configuration, and administration of Workstation, though much of the material will work on Server as well. The Server Resource Kit focuses mostly on domain and Enterprise networking issues and how NT Server fits into that picture. Both kits come with a CD-ROM full of helpful utilities. We’ve heard some people joke that NT is the add-on companion product for the Resource Kit. What they’re trying to say is that anyone who is serious about supporting Windows 2000 will want access to the information in both kits.

Summary

No matter what your interest is in Windows 2000, there will be times when understanding the process of troubleshooting will be helpful to you. Even if troubleshooting is never going to be part of your responsibility, things may go wrong. If you have even a basic understanding of the process, you will convey much more meaningful information to the person who is responsible for the troubleshooting.

Use the methods listed in this chapter when troubleshooting your own computer. Follow the guidelines for documenting the steps taken and the possible approaches to use. Always remember to think simple at first, and then move on to the more exotic as you eliminate basic possibilities.

Take the time to become familiar with the Microsoft Knowledge Base. It can help you to find solutions to your own computer problems without spending additional money or time on support. If you are a support professional, the KB provides the raw information needed to excel in your job.
