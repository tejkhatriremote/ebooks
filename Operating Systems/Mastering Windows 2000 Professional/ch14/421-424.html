Creating a Cover Page

Are you finding the cover pages provided by Windows 2000 to be a little dull? They just don’t seem to reflect your sense of taste and style? Maybe you want something a little flashier? This is your section, then. In this section, you will learn how to create a new custom cover page to use with your faxes.

The process starts in Control Panel with the Fax applet. On the Cover Pages tab of the Fax Properties dialog box, you will see a New button that will open the Cover Page Editor. (Remember this from earlier in this chapter?) That’s where we’re going now. Use the following steps to open the Cover Page Editor:

1.  Open the Fax Properties by double-clicking the Fax icon in Control Panel.
2.  Click the Cover Pages tab, and then click the New button to open the Cover Page Editor shown in Figure 14.14.


Figure 14.14  The Cover Page Editor lets you create your own custom cover pages.

3.  Click OK on the Tips dialog box to close it. The Tips are worth reading through if you are new to the Fax Service and editing cover pages, but we’ll pass on them for now.
4.  The Cover Page Editor is now ready to work with a blank document already loaded for use.

The Cover Page Editor looks like a simple word processor, such as WordPad, at first glance. But if you browse through the menus and toolbars a little, you’ll soon discover that the editor is more akin to a desktop publishing program. The Cover Page Editor is more concerned with placement of fields on the page and the overall layout and appearance of the page than with entering text. Let’s take a closer look at some of the capabilities of this editor.

The Basics of Cover Pages

There are certain bits of information that any cover page should have: the name of the recipient, the fax number of the recipient, the name and number of the sender, and so on. Let’s start our exploration of the Cover Page Editor with a look at adding these basic fields, and then we’ll move on to some of the more advanced features.

First, it might be a good idea to make it easier to place the fields evenly on the page. The editor includes an option to turn on a grid view to make placement easier. To turn on the grid view select the Grid Lines command from the View menu.

The Insert menu contains commands that will place the common fields on your cover page. The fields are dropped into the center of the page, and you will need to drag them to their final destination. The fields are divided into three groups on the menu: Recipient, Sender, and Message fields.

The Recipient menu contains fields for the person’s name and their fax number. You will most likely want to include these in your cover page unless you’re certain that the recipient is the only person at that number.

The Sender menu has a few more options to include. You can mix and match until you find the balance that’s right for you. A good minimum might be to include your name and number, though your company name, office location, and department also might be useful to include.

The Message menu only has four options for fields to include. They are probably all good to include, but a bare minimum would be a Subject field and a Date and Time Sent field. Most cover pages include all four of these options as standard practice. Again, you’ll have to decide what works best for you.

Once you have inserted the fields that you want to include, you can drag them anywhere you want on the page. In Figure 14.15, you can see that the Subject fields are highlighted with drag handles at the corners. These handles signify that the fields can be moved by clicking anywhere in them and dragging them to their new position. The handles can also be used to resize the fields.


Figure 14.15  You can easily move any of the fields on your cover page.

To select multiple fields at one time, click somewhere near the outermost field in the group, then, holding the mouse button down, drag the cursor until you’ve selected all of the fields in the group. When you let go of the mouse button, all of those fields will be selected and can be moved as a unit.
